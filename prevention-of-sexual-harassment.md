# Prevention of Sexual Harassment

## Q.1 What kinds of behaviour cause sexual harassment?
### Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome behavior of a sexual nature that makes a person feel uncomfortable, unsafe, intimidated, or offended.

Examples include:

- Unwanted physical contact or advances.
- Sexual comments, jokes, or remarks.
- Sending inappropriate messages, images, or videos.
- Repeated requests for dates despite refusal.
- Staring, gestures, or behavior with sexual intent.
- Offering benefits or making threats in exchange for sexual favors.
- Creating a hostile, humiliating, or offensive work environment.


## Q.2 What would you do if you face or witness any incident or repeated incidents of such behaviour?
### If I face or witness sexual harassment, I would:

1. Ensure my safety and remain calm.
2. Clearly communicate that the behavior is unwelcome, if it is safe to do so.
3. Document the incident, including the date, time, location, and any witnesses.
4. Report the incident to my manager, HR, or the Internal Committee (IC) according to the PoSH policy.
5. Support the affected person and encourage them to report the incident.
6. Cooperate with the investigation process and maintain confidentiality.
7. Report repeated incidents immediately, even if they appear minor individually.

Everyone is responsible for maintaining a respectful, safe, and harassment-free workplace.
