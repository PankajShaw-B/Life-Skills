# Learning Process

## Question 1
### What is the Feynman Technique? Explain in 1 line.
The Feynman Technique is a learning method where you master a concept by explaining it in the simplest possible terms, as if you were teaching it to a child.

## Question 2
### In this video, what was the most interesting story or idea for you?
For me, the absolute most fascinating part of Barbara Oakley's talk is the story about how Thomas Edison and Salvador Dalí used to brainstorm. It’s such a brilliant, slightly weird trick.

Oakley explains that our brains have two modes: a tight, intense "focus" mode, and a relaxed, wandering "diffuse" mode. We usually get stuck on hard problems because we are focusing too hard and getting trapped in the same old thought patterns.

To break out of this, Edison would sit in a comfy chair with heavy metal ball bearings in his hand and let his mind wander as he started to drift off. The exact second he fell asleep, his hand would relax, the bearings would crash to the floor, and he'd wake up instantly. He would immediately write down whatever wild, creative ideas his relaxed brain had just been playing with before he could forget them.

It’s just a great reminder that sometimes the best way to figure out a tough problem isn’t to force it, but to literally just step back, relax, and let your brain do its thing in the background.

## Question 3
### What are active and diffused modes of thinking?
* **Focused Mode (Active):** This is when you are concentrating hard on a specific task. Your brain uses tight, established pathways. It is like a flashlight with a narrow, bright beam, or a pinball machine where the bumpers are very close together. You use this for familiar step-by-step tasks, like solving a math problem you already know how to do.
* **Diffuse Mode (Relaxed):** This is when your mind is relaxed and allowed to wander (like when you are in the shower, walking, or falling asleep). Your brain makes wide, big-picture connections. It is like a flashlight with a broad, soft beam, or a pinball machine with bumpers far apart. You need this mode to have "aha!" moments and understand completely new concepts.

## Question 5
### What are some of the actions you can take going forward to improve your learning process?
### Here is the best advice I can give you:

* **Lock in.** Silence your phone, block the distractions, and give the work your full attention. Work hard when it's time to work, so you can relax guilt-free later.
* **Don't just memorize.** When you tackle a new concept, get curious. Figure out *how* to build it. If you can explain it to someone else in plain English, you truly get it. 
* **Speak up.** Don't spin your wheels in silence. If you hit a roadblock, ask for help early. Being a great developer is just as much about communicating clearly as it is about writing code.
