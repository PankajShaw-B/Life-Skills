# Learning Assertive Communication 

## Question 1
### What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? (Minimum 6 points)
### Active Listening Strategies

* **Be Present:** Give your full attention and stop internal multitasking.
* **Show Interest:** Use eye contact, nodding, and an open posture.
* **Paraphrase:** Repeat back what you heard in your own words to check for accuracy.
* **Ask Questions:** Use "how" or "what" questions to encourage the speaker to share more.
* **Listen for Feeling:** Pay attention to the speaker's tone and body language, not just their words.
* **Don't Interrupt:** Wait until the speaker is finished before you offer a response.

## Question 2
### According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? (Write in your own words, use simple English)
### Reflective Listening (Fisher's Model)

* **Stay Neutral:** Listen without judging or deciding who is right or wrong.
* **Mirror the Content:** Repeat the main facts back in your own words to show you understand.
* **Reflect Feelings:** Identify the emotions behind the words (e.g., "It sounds like you feel frustrated").
* **Clarify:** Ask simple questions if you don't understand a specific point.
* **Check Accuracy:** Ask "Did I get that right?" to make sure your understanding is correct.
* **Encourage:** Use small prompts like "Go on" or "I see" to keep the speaker talking.

## Question 3
### What are the obstacles in your listening process?
### Obstacles to Effective Listening

* **Internal Distractions:** Your mind wanders to your to-do list or what you want to say next.
* **Environmental Noise:** Loud sounds, phones, or people nearby make it hard to focus.
* **Personal Bias:** Judging the speaker or their ideas before they even finish talking.
* **Emotional Triggers:** Getting upset or defensive, which causes you to stop processing facts.
* **Information Overload:** Trying to take in too much complex information at once.
* **Assumptions:** Thinking you already know what the speaker is going to say.

## Question 4
### What can you do to improve your listening?
### Ways to Improve Your Listening

* **Minimize Distractions:** Silence your phone and find a quiet space to talk.
* **Stop Rehearsing:** Focus entirely on the speaker instead of planning your reply.
* **Practice Mindfulness:** Catch your mind when it wanders and gently bring it back to the conversation.
* **Use Body Language:** Lean in and keep eye contact to stay physically engaged in the moment.
* **Wait for the Pause:** Count to two after the speaker finishes before you start talking.
* **Ask for Feedback:** Occasionally ask the speaker, "Am I understanding you clearly?" to stay on track.

## Question 5
### When do you switch to a passive communication style in your day-to-day life?
### When Passive Communication Happens

* **To Avoid Conflict:** Choosing to stay silent or agree to prevent an argument or "keep the peace."
* **Low Importance Situations:** When you don't actually care about the outcome, such as picking a place for lunch.
* **In Power Imbalances:** Remaining quiet when talking to a strict boss or someone in a high position of authority.
* **Feeling Overwhelmed:** Switching to passive mode when you are too tired or stressed to explain your own needs.
* **Lack of Confidence:** Staying silent because you feel your ideas aren't good enough or you fear being judged.
* **Cultural or Social Norms:** Being passive out of politeness or to show respect in certain formal settings.

## Question 6
### When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?
### When Aggressive Communication Happens

* **Under High Stress:** Losing patience and snapping at others when feeling overwhelmed or rushed.
* **When Feeling Unheard:** Raising your voice or using harsh words because you feel ignored or disrespected.
* **During Heated Conflicts:** Switching to a "win at all costs" mindset during an argument to protect your ego.
* **Perceived Threats:** Reacting defensively and attacking others when you feel your work or character is being criticized.
* **To Gain Control:** Using a demanding tone to force a quick decision or to take charge of a chaotic situation.
* **Physical Exhaustion:** Having a "short fuse" and reacting with anger due to lack of sleep or hunger.

## Question 7
### When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day-to-day life?
### When Passive-Aggressive Communication Happens

* **Fear of Direct Confrontation:** Using sarcasm or hints because you feel uncomfortable addressing an issue directly.
* **Feeling Powerless:** Resorting to the "silent treatment" or gossiping when you feel you can't change a situation openly.
* **Hidden Resentment:** Making small taunts or "jokes" about someone because of an unresolved past problem.
* **Avoiding Responsibility:** Using subtle excuses or "forgetting" tasks to show your frustration without saying it.
* **Protecting Yourself:** Using sarcasm as a shield to avoid showing true vulnerability or being taken seriously.
* **Social Pressures:** Gossiping about a peer when you feel competitive but want to maintain a "nice" public image.

## Question 8
### How can you make your communication assertive? You can watch and analyse the videos, then think what would be a few steps you can apply in your own life? (Watch the videos first before answering this question.)
### How to Be Assertive

* **Use "I" Statements:** Say "I feel..." instead of "You always..." to avoid sounding like you are attacking.
* **Know Your Goal:** Figure out exactly what you need before you start talking.
* **Practice on Small Things:** Start by making small requests in safe situations to build your confidence.
* **Check Your Tone:** Keep your voice calm and steady—not too soft (passive) and not shouting (aggressive).
* **Stand Tall:** Use good eye contact and a relaxed posture to show you are serious but calm.
* **Speak Up Early:** Don't wait until you are angry; address small issues before they get big.
